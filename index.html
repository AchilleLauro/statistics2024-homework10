Sampling Mean and Variance, and Lebesgue–Stieltjes Integration

An overview of fundamental concepts in statistics and measure theory.

General Concept of Sampling Mean and Variance

1. Sampling Mean (\( \bar{X} \))

The sampling mean is a measure of central tendency, calculated as the arithmetic average of a set of sample observations.

Formula:

\[ \bar{X} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \]

Distribution of the Sampling Mean:

  • Mean: \( \mu \) (the population mean).
  • Variance: \( \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \), where \( \sigma^2 \) is the population variance.
  • Shape: Approaches a normal distribution as the sample size \( n \) increases, according to the Central Limit Theorem.

2. Sampling Variance (\( S^2 \))

The sampling variance measures the dispersion of sample observations around the sample mean. It provides an estimate of the population variance.

Formula:

\[ S^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^n (X_i - \bar{X})^2 \]

Distribution of the Sampling Variance:

  • Mean: \( \sigma^2 \) (an unbiased estimator of the population variance).
  • Distribution: For a normal population, the scaled statistic \((n-1) \frac{S^2}{\sigma^2}\) follows a chi-square distribution with \( n-1 \) degrees of freedom.

Main Features of Their Distributions

Sample Mean:

  • Mean: \( \mu \)
  • Variance: \( \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \)
  • Shape: Approaches normality as \( n \) increases.

Sample Variance:

  • Mean: \( \sigma^2 \)
  • Distribution: Chi-square distribution with \( n-1 \) degrees of freedom (when the population is normal).

Lebesgue–Stieltjes Integration

General Idea

The Lebesgue–Stieltjes integral extends the concepts of the Riemann and Lebesgue integrals, enabling integration with respect to functions of bounded variation. This generalization provides a flexible framework for integration in probability and measure theory.

Applications in Probability Theory

  • Expectation as an Integral: The expected value of a random variable \( X \) can be expressed using the Lebesgue–Stieltjes integral as: \[ \mathbb{E}[X] = \int_{-\infty}^\infty x \, dF(x) \] where \( F(x) \) is the cumulative distribution function (CDF) of \( X \).
  • Integration by Parts: In probability, this property simplifies the computation of expectations for products of random variables: \[ \int_a^b u \, dv = uv \big|_a^b - \int_a^b v \, du \]

Applications in Measure Theory

  • Measure Construction: A function \( g \) of bounded variation defines a Lebesgue–Stieltjes measure \( \mu_g \). This is used to assign measures to intervals, facilitating integration with respect to \( g \).
  • Extension of Integration Theory: The Lebesgue–Stieltjes integral extends the Lebesgue integral to allow integration with respect to more general measures, such as those induced by cumulative distribution functions.
