General Concept of Sampling Mean and Variance

Sampling Mean (ùëãÃÑ)

  • Definition: The arithmetic average of sample observations.
  • Distribution: According to the Central Limit Theorem, for a sufficiently large sample size \( n \), the sampling distribution of the mean approaches a normal distribution with mean \( \mu \) and variance \( \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \).

Sampling Variance (ùëÜ¬≤)

  • Definition: Measures the dispersion of sample observations around the sample mean, calculated as \[ S^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^n (X_i - \bar{X})^2 \].
  • Distribution: When sampling from a normal distribution, the statistic \((n-1) \frac{S^2}{\sigma^2}\) follows a chi-square distribution with \( n-1 \) degrees of freedom.

Main Features of Their Distributions

  • Sample Mean:
    • Mean: \( \mu \)
    • Variance: \( \frac{\sigma^2}{n} \)
    • Shape: Approaches normality as \( n \) increases (Central Limit Theorem).
  • Sample Variance:
    • Mean: \( \sigma^2 \) (unbiased estimator)
    • Distribution: Scaled chi-square distribution for normal populations.

General Idea

The Lebesgue‚ÄìStieltjes integral integrates a function \( f \) with respect to a function \( g \) of bounded variation, generalizing integration to a broader class of functions and measures.

Applications in Probability Theory

  • Expectation as an Integral: The expected value of a random variable \( X \) can be expressed as: \[ \mathbb{E}[X] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x \, dF(x) \] where \( F \) is the cumulative distribution function (CDF) of \( X \).
  • Integration by Parts: Useful for computing expectations involving products of random variables.

Applications in Measure Theory

  • Measure Construction: Any function of bounded variation on the real line defines a Lebesgue‚ÄìStieltjes measure.
  • Extension of Integration Theory: Extends the Lebesgue integral to more general measures, enhancing flexibility and applicability.
